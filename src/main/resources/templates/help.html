Mastermind is a "code-breaking" game. One player (in this case the computer) comes up with a secret "code" - a row of colored pegs. The other player (that's you) tries to guess the combination. After each guess, the computer will give you hints about how close you are to the correct combination. Specifically:
  • For each colored peg in the guess that exactly matches the secret code, you will get one black pin.
  • For the other pegs, you will get one gray pin for each color match.
Let's look at an example.
Secret: Red - Yellow - Blue - Green (Note: you will never see this in the actual game. It's secret after all!)
Guess: Blue - Yellow - Green - Orange

Right Color, Wrong Spot: 2
Right Color, Right spot: 1


Here we got one exact match - the yellow peg in spot 2. We also got two colors that are correct, but in the wrong spot (Green and Blue). The computer would show 2 gray pins and 1 black pin - pretty good for our first guess! Then we'd make another guess, and receive more feedback, and so on until we either run out of guesses (10 by default) or correctly guess the pattern. Note that the order of the pins does not correspond to anything; it's just the number of pegs that matters.

Good luck!
